Secondary Sources

Author Title Year Type Summary
Khazaei, F., Ahmad Kaker, S., Fleetwood, J., & Pfingst, A. Visualising Truth to Power: A Response to Forensic Architecture (2024) A Cartography of Genocide: Israel's Conduct in Gaza since October 2023 2025 Peer-reviewed Review Essay Provides a scholarly review of A Cartography of Genocide, arguing that the project expands the role of architectural and spatial evidence in documenting mass violence. The authors praise its contribution to legal accountability while also discussing methodological limitations and ethical questions surrounding the interpretation of visual evidence.
Giordano, Alberto Cartographies of Genocide 2019 Conference Paper Examines how cartographic methods are used to document genocide and preserve evidence. Although it predates this investigation, the paper offers theoretical concepts that help evaluate the mapping strategies employed in A Cartography of Genocide.
Piscitelli, Davide Cartografia di un genocidio 2025 Interview / Critical Commentary Discusses the investigation's interdisciplinary methodology, combining architecture, satellite imagery and open-source intelligence. The commentary highlights the project's strengths in evidence gathering while reflecting on the practical challenges of translating complex spatial data into legal and public narratives.
