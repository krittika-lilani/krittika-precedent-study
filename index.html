Secondary Sources

Author Title Year Type Summary (Review, Appraisal & Criticism)
Khazaei, Ahmad Kaker, Fleetwood & Pfingst Visualising Truth to Power: A Response to Forensic Architecture (2024) A Cartography of Genocide: Israel's Conduct in Gaza since October 2023 2025 Peer-reviewed Review Essay Reviews the investigation as an innovative model of visual and spatial evidence production. The authors appraise its contribution to documenting mass violence and supporting legal accountability, while also critically reflecting on the strengths, limitations, and ethical challenges of using visual investigations as forensic evidence.
Alberto Giordano Cartographies of Genocide 2019 Conference Paper Although published before this investigation, the paper provides theoretical context by examining how maps function in documenting and communicating genocide. It offers a conceptual framework for understanding the cartographic methods employed by A Cartography of Genocide rather than evaluating the project directly.
Davide Piscitelli Cartografia di un genocidio 2025 Interview / Critical Commentary Examines the project's methodology, discussing how architectural analysis, satellite imagery, and open-source evidence are combined to construct legal arguments. The commentary appraises the project's interdisciplinary approach while considering its broader implications for contemporary human-rights investigations.
